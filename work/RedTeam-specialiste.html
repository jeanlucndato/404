(Methodology)

My operations follow the **Kill Chain and MITRE ATT&CK frameworks** to emulate realistic threat actors. The process begins with extensive **OSINT** (Open-Source Intelligence) and **reconnaissance** to identify the initial vector, which often involves customized phishing or exploiting zero-day vulnerabilities. The objective is to achieve a **low-and-slow infiltration** to avoid detection by the Blue Team's defensive stack. This phase demands advanced evasion techniques, including fileless execution and sandbox bypass, ensuring **initial access** and C2 channel establishment remains stealthy.
Upon establishing a foothold, the focus shifts to **privilege escalation** and methodical **lateral movement** across the internal network, often targeting weak configurations in Active Directory or cloud environments. This leads to the final stage: achieving the **Critical Mission Objective** (CMO), such as exfiltrating sensitive data or gaining domain administrator access. The operation concludes not just with an exploit, but with a **comprehensive report and Purple Team debriefing** to enhance the client's detection and response capabilities.
